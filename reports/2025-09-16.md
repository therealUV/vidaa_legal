### Briefing (~200 words)

The key themes across the items include leadership transitions, regulatory updates, and judicial clarifications within the European banking framework.

1. **Leadership Transition**: The resignation of José Manuel Campa as Chairperson of the EBA [1] signifies a shift in governance that may influence the authority's operational effectiveness and strategic direction. The transition process is set to conclude by January 2026, indicating a structured approach to leadership change.

2. **Regulatory Updates**: The EBA's issuance of revised validation rules [2] aims to enhance the supervisory reporting process by deactivating inaccurate rules. This change is immediate and affects all financial institutions, reflecting a commitment to improving data accuracy and compliance.

3. **Judicial Clarifications**: The General Court's orders in cases T-640/17 [3], T-517/17 [4], and T-660/17 [5] underscore the evolving legal landscape concerning appeals against SRB decisions. These rulings emphasize the need for transparency and timely communication from the SRB, establishing new obligations that impact stakeholders involved in bank resolution processes.

Overall, these developments highlight a concerted effort to improve governance, regulatory clarity, and judicial oversight within the EU banking sector, with implications for financial institutions and regulatory bodies alike.
