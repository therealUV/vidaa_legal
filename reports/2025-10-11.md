### Briefing (~200 words)

The key themes across the items [1], [2], [3], [4], and [5] highlight significant regulatory changes impacting financial institutions and their operations within the evolving landscape of digital currencies and asset management.

Item [1] emphasizes the financial stability implications of the digital euro, mandating banks to assess and report on investment costs associated with its implementation. This underscores the need for preparedness among stakeholders to mitigate potential risks to financial stability.

Item [2] addresses proposed changes to liquidity requirements under the MiCA framework, indicating a potential misalignment with existing prudential standards. This could alter obligations for crypto-asset service providers, necessitating adjustments in compliance timelines as discussions between the EBA and EC progress.

Items [3], [4], and [5] focus on the ECB's amendments to the corporate sector purchase programme, which enhance eligibility criteria for corporate bonds and introduce compliance obligations related to environmental, social, and governance (ESG) standards. These changes aim to broaden access for issuers while reinforcing transparency and accountability among participating financial institutions. The immediate implementation timeline for these amendments indicates a swift transition for affected parties.

Overall, these items reflect a trend towards increased regulatory scrutiny and the integration of sustainability criteria in financial practices, necessitating proactive adaptation by financial institutions.
