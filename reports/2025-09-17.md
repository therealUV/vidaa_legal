# EUR-Lex Daily Digest — 2025-09-17

## Executive Summary

### Key Items
- [1] The sixth Anti-Money Laundering Directive aims to enhance mechanisms for preventing money laundering and terrorist financing across EU Member States.
- [2] The new Anti-Money Laundering Regulation aims to enhance the EU's framework for preventing money laundering and terrorist financing.
- [3] The sixth Anti-Money Laundering Directive updates and repeals Directive (EU) 2015/849, enhancing measures against money laundering and terrorist financing.
- [4] The new Anti-Money Laundering Regulation aims to enhance the EU's framework for preventing money laundering and terrorist financing.
- [5] The European Parliament's resolution outlines estimates for revenue and expenditure for the financial year 2025, reflecting updated fiscal priorities.

### Briefing (~200 words)

The key themes across the items [1], [2], [3], and [4] focus on the European Union's efforts to strengthen its framework against money laundering and terrorist financing. Both the sixth Anti-Money Laundering Directive and the new Anti-Money Laundering Regulation introduce stricter obligations for financial institutions and non-financial entities, emphasizing enhanced due diligence and reporting requirements. The scope of these regulations has expanded to include virtual assets and service providers, reflecting the evolving nature of financial transactions.

Member States are mandated to transpose these directives and regulations into national law within specified timelines, ensuring uniform compliance across the EU. Affected parties include banks, financial institutions, real estate agents, and various non-financial sectors, all of which must adapt to the updated regulatory landscape.

In contrast, item [5] addresses the European Parliament's estimates of revenue and expenditure for the financial year 2025, highlighting fiscal priorities and budgetary constraints. This resolution emphasizes the importance of transparency in financial reporting and ongoing evaluations of budget allocations, which may impact social programs, environmental initiatives, and digital transformation efforts.

Overall, these items reflect a comprehensive approach to enhancing financial integrity and accountability within the EU, while also addressing broader fiscal responsibilities.
