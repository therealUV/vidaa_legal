- The paper examines the interaction between EU banking prudential and resolution frameworks, highlighting new challenges in capital buffer usability.
- It reveals that overlaps between risk-based and leverage-based requirements reduce buffer usability to 65-74%, and further to 40-50% when resolution frameworks are included.
- Implementation of final Basel III standards is suggested to enhance buffer usability significantly.
- Resolution requirements will be fully phased in by 2024, affecting banks' capital targets and balance sheet structures.
- Affected parties include banks, macroprudential authorities, and stakeholders in the EU banking system, facing new obligations and potential trade-offs in capital management.
