Overall, these items underscore the interconnectedness of regulatory frameworks, economic forecasting, and the influence of monetary policy on corporate finance, suggesting a need for enhanced clarity and consideration of specific risks in financial decision-making.

**[4] [Firms’ risk and monetary transmission: revisiting the excess bond premium](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp3118~179c0cfd60.en.pdf)**

- The study extends the excess bond premium (EBP) framework to incorporate firm-specific cyclical and idiosyncratic risks affecting corporate bond spreads.
- It reveals that monetary policy shocks significantly influence credit spreads through changes in default risk compensation, especially for firms with cyclical risk.
- For cyclical firms, up to 25% of spread widening after contractionary monetary policy is attributed to increased default risk compensation.
- Firms with high idiosyncratic risk do not exhibit a notable differential response to monetary policy changes compared to others.
- The findings suggest a need for investors to consider firm-specific risk profiles when assessing bond spreads and monetary policy impacts.
