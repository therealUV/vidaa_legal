# EUR-Lex Daily Digest — 2025-09-08

## Executive Summary

### Key Items
- [1] The General Court ruled on the calculation of 2019 ex ante contributions by the Single Resolution Board (SRB) for the Single Resolution Fund (SRF).
- [2] The General Court ruled on the legality of the SRB's 2019 ex ante contributions calculation for the Single Resolution Fund.
- [3] The General Court ruled on June 11, 2025, regarding DZ Bank's annulment action against the Single Resolution Board (SRB).
- [4] The General Court's judgment on 23 July 2025 upheld the freezing of funds for individuals/entities linked to actions threatening Ukraine's integrity.
- [5] The General Court ruled on the case concerning the freezing of funds and entry restrictions related to Syria, impacting individuals and entities on the EU's sanctions list.

### Briefing (~200 words)

The key themes across the items [1], [2], [3], [4], and [5] revolve around legal principles in the context of the European Union's regulatory framework, particularly concerning the banking sector and sanctions policy.

In cases [1] and [2], the General Court addressed the Single Resolution Board's (SRB) calculations for ex ante contributions to the Single Resolution Fund (SRF). Both rulings emphasized the obligation to provide reasons for decisions, the right to effective judicial protection, and the principles of equal treatment and proportionality. The court's findings highlighted the need for transparency in regulatory actions affecting financial institutions.

Case [3] further clarified the jurisdiction of the General Court regarding annulment actions under the Single Resolution Mechanism (SRM), ruling DZ Bank's action inadmissible due to a lack of direct concern. This underscores the limitations of judicial recourse for financial institutions under specific regulatory frameworks.

In cases [4] and [5], the court dealt with sanctions related to foreign policy, focusing on the freezing of assets and entry restrictions. Both judgments stressed the importance of proportionality and the rights of defense, particularly in relation to individual circumstances, such as family ties. These rulings illustrate the balance between enforcing sanctions and respecting individual rights, reinforcing the legal framework governing EU foreign policy measures.
