## Executive Summary

### Key Items
- [1] Introduces regulatory technical standards for applications to offer asset-referenced tokens to the public or for trading admission.
- [2] New regulatory standards introduced for applications to offer asset-referenced tokens to the public or for trading admission.
- [3] Introduces regulatory technical standards for applications to offer asset-referenced tokens to the public or for trading admission.

- [5] The review of the securitisation regulation introduces updated definitions and criteria for qualifying securitisations, enhancing clarity and consistency across EU member states.

### Briefing (~200 words)

The key themes across the items relate to the introduction of regulatory frameworks aimed at enhancing transparency, consumer protection, and compliance within the financial markets, particularly concerning digital assets and securitisation.

1. **Regulatory Standards and Compliance**: Items [1], [2], and [3] emphasize the establishment of regulatory technical standards for asset-referenced tokens, mandating comprehensive disclosures about token characteristics, risks, and governance. This aims to ensure that issuers and financial institutions adhere to stringent compliance timelines and requirements under Regulation (EU) 2023/1114.

2. **Transparency and Consumer Protection**: The focus on detailed information requirements across the regulations highlights a commitment to improving transparency in financial markets. This is particularly relevant for consumers and investors, who will benefit from clearer insights into the risks and structures of asset-referenced tokens and securitisation products.

3. **Broader Scope and Stakeholder Impact**: The implications extend to a wide range of stakeholders, including issuers, financial institutions, and regulatory bodies, as seen in items [4] and [5]. The Competitiveness Council's discussions on the Competitiveness Fund and the review of the securitisation regulation indicate a broader regulatory landscape that encompasses various sectors, necessitating new obligations for member states and stakeholders.

4. **Implementation Timelines**: Each item outlines specific timelines for compliance and implementation, indicating a structured approach to rolling out these regulations, which will require stakeholders to adapt to new requirements within set periods.
