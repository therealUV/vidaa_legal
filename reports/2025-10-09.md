**[5] [Decoding climate-related risks in sovereign bond pricing: a global perspective](https://www.ecb.europa.eu//pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp3135~024090a8b9.en.pdf)**

- The study investigates the impact of climate risks on sovereign yields across 52 economies over two decades, highlighting the distinction between transition and physical risks.
- Transition risk leads to higher sovereign yields, particularly in developing and high-emission countries post-Paris Agreement, while high-temperature anomalies do not significantly affect borrowing costs.
- Countries with high debt levels experience increased sovereign yields as acute physical risks rise.
- The analysis reveals varying responses of sovereign yields to different climate-related disasters, influenced by income levels and fiscal capacity.
- The findings suggest a need for policymakers to consider climate risks in financial stability assessments and sovereign debt management strategies.
